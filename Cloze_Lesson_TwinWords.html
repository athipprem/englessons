Why this matters 🌍

Some joining words are twins — they mean the same thing but go in different places. "so" and "as a result" both show a result; "although" and "however" both show a surprise. Choosing the right twin makes your writing flow correctly — and it's the one cloze thing that keeps tripping you. Once you know the trick, these become free marks.

The one thing to fix 🎯

Last time you got the job right every time (you knew it was a RESULT) — but you sometimes put the wrong twin in the gap (so where as a result belonged). Good news: the punctuation tells you which twin, every single time. Let's lock it. Next →

The whole trick
First decide the job (which row). Then let the punctuation pick the column: full stop → the starter twin, comma → the joiner twin. Same meaning, the mark before the gap chooses the word.

Drill 1 — pick the right twin ⚡

Same job both times — just choose the twin that fits the punctuation.

1. "The rain poured down. ____, the picnic was cancelled." full stop
2. "The rain poured down, ____ the picnic was cancelled." comma
3. "He studied hard, ____ he still felt unsure." comma
4. "He studied hard. ____, he still felt unsure." full stop
5. "Dad washed the car ____ Mum mowed the lawn." mid-sentence
6. "Dad washed the car. ____, Mum mowed the lawn." full stop

Drill 2 — the sandcastle cloze 🏖️

Job first, then punctuation picks the twin. Decide once, box it, move on.

Our team entered the sandcastle competition, (1)____ we practised digging all week. The tide was creeping in (2)____ we built the tall towers. Our castle looked amazing. (3)____, a big wave knocked off the top. We rebuilt it quickly. (4)____, the judges still gave us a high score. (5)____, the team next to us was only just starting their moat.

Gap 1 — "entered the competition, ____ we practised all week"
Gap 2 — "the tide was creeping in ____ we built the towers"
Gap 3 — "looked amazing. ____, a big wave knocked off the top"
Gap 4 — "rebuilt it quickly. ____, the judges gave us a high score"
Gap 5 — "____, the team next to us was only just starting their moat"
Job first, then the mark before the gap picks the twin:
full stop → as a result / however / meanwhile  ·  comma → so / although / while.
