What is an "if" sentence?

An "if" sentence imagines something that is not actually happening, and says what would follow. It has two halves:

If the dog had wings,  |  it would fly over the fence.
↑ the "if" half (the imagined idea)  ·  ↑ the result half (what would follow)

Here's the one thing that matters: both halves must belong to the same "world". There are only two worlds. Let's meet them. Next →

The two worlds 🌍🌗

🌍 Maybe World

About now / always — it could still happen. Imagining a different "now".

If half: had + a thing → "had a pool", "had wings"

Result: would + verb → "would swim", "would fly"

🌗 Too-Late World

About the past — it's finished, too late to change.

If half: had + a done-word → "had been locked", "had fixed"

Result: would have + verb → "would have stayed"

"A thing" vs "a done-word": a thing is a noun (a pool, wings, a sign). A done-word is a past action (been locked, fixed, left). That one difference tells you the world.

The Decision Tree 🌳

START — look at the if half
↓
"had a ___" + would + verb?
YES ↓
🌍 Maybe World
NO — "had + done-word" + would have? ↓
🌗 Too-Late World
↓
Both halves SAME world? ✓ correct  |  Different worlds? ✗ that's the error
One golden rule: the "Will Ban"
The result half NEVER uses "will". If you see "will" after the comma, it's wrong — every time.

Drill 1 — Which world is this half? 🔎

Read each half. had + thing or would + verb = Maybe. had + done-word or would have = Too-Late.

1. "the school had a swimming pool"
2. "the team would have won the match"
3. "we would travel more often"
4. "the door had been locked"
5. "the garden had a little pond"
6. "they would have arrived on time"

Drill 2 — Do the two halves match? 🧦

Label each half, then decide: same world = correct ✓, different worlds = error ✗ (the odd socks).

1. "If the park had a fountain, children would play there all day."
2. "If the bus had left earlier, we would have caught the train."
3. "If the shop had a sign, more people would have noticed it."
4. "If the roof had been fixed, the books would have stayed dry."
5. "If the town had a pool, everyone would have swum every summer."

Keep-sharp — quick vocab ✨

You're great at vocab — just keeping it warm. Pick the meaning that the clue proves.

1. "After the long hike, Ben was exhausted and could barely lift his feet."
2. "Mia was delighted when she opened the present and squealed with joy."
3. "The mule was stubborn and refused to move no matter how hard they pulled."
Label both halves → same world = correct, different = error.
And "will" after the comma is always wrong.
