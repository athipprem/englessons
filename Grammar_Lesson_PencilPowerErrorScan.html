Why this matters 🔍

"Which sentence has an error?" questions show up on every real test you'll ever take — ISA, ASMOPSS, school exams, all of them. Today we change HOW we practise this one, because the last approach didn't stick without a screen helping.

Before We Start — quick check on what's still solid 🎉

Gerund/infinitive after "regret" stayed correct again last test — quick confirm it's still automatic.

1. "Looking back now, he always regretted ___ (quit) the swim team so early." Which form?
🔎 What actually happened last test — an honest look

On every single error-scan question you got wrong last test, you picked one of the CORRECT, matching sentences — never once the actual broken one. That's an important clue: clicking to reveal labels on-screen helped WHILE you were doing it, but that helper isn't there on the real test, so the habit didn't come with you. Today we build the exact same routine using nothing but your own pencil and paper — the same kind of fix that cracked the very first grammar check in this whole programme, years ago.

The new rule: pencil, not clicking ✏️

The routine itself hasn't changed: label the IF-half (THIRD-shape "had ___ed" or SECOND-shape plain past), then label the RESULT-half ("would have ___ed" = THIRD, plain "would ___" = SECOND). If the two labels don't match, that option is broken.

From today — on practice AND on the real test — grab a pencil and paper. For every option, write "3rd" or "2nd" above the IF-half, then write "3rd" or "2nd" above the RESULT-half. Only after all four options are labelled on YOUR paper do you choose an answer.
🔑 Why your own pencil beats clicking

Clicking a button to reveal a label does the "stop and look" step FOR you. Writing it yourself forces YOUR eyes to actually do the checking, with nothing else doing it for you — which is exactly the condition you'll be in on test day. It's slower at first. That's expected. It gets fast with reps, the same way the Had Test drill did.

Watch the pencil routine, step by step 👀

Option 1: "If the janitor had locked the gym early, the team would have practised in the hallway instead." Pencil above IF-half: 3rd ("had locked"). Pencil above RESULT-half: 3rd ("would have practised"). Match → clean.

Option 2: "If the scoreboard had stopped working, the referee would call the time out loud instead." Pencil: IF-half 3rd ("had stopped"). RESULT-half 2nd (plain "would call"). Labels DON'T match → this is the error.

Option 3: "If the whistle had been left at home, the coach would have used a loud clap instead." IF-half: 3rd. RESULT-half: 3rd. Match → clean.

Option 4: "If the bleachers had been wet, the crowd would have stood along the fence instead." IF-half: 3rd. RESULT-half: 3rd. Match → clean.

Answer: Option 2 — the only pair of pencil labels that didn't match. Notice every option got labelled first, even the clean ones — that's the whole point of writing it down instead of guessing by feel.

Pencil drill — 10 rounds 🏷️

"If the school's music room ___ soundproofed last year, the choir practice ___ so much noise for the whole hallway right now." Choose the correct pair.

Great effort! 🎉

On every error-scan question — practice AND the real test — write "2nd" or "3rd" above each half of every option with your own pencil before you choose. If the labels match, it's clean. If they don't, that's your error.
