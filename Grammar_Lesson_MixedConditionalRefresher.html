Mixed Conditionals — a past cause, a right-now result 🕰️

You already know two of the four conditional types really well. A quick reminder of both, side by side:

SECOND — dream about NOWTHIRD — regret the PAST
If + past simple, ... would + verb
"If I had more time, I would help."
(the cause AND the result are both about now/the unreal present)		If + had + past participle, ... would have + verb
"If I had had more time, I would have helped."
(the cause AND the result are both about the past)

A Mixed Conditional borrows HALF from each — a cause that really happened in the PAST, but a result you can still see or feel RIGHT NOW. That's why it's called "mixed": it crosses two different times in one sentence.

MIXED — past cause, present result
If + had (not) + past participle (borrowed from THIRD — the cause is really in the past) ... would (not) + verb (borrowed from SECOND — the result is about right now)
"If the roof had been repaired last year, the ceiling would not be leaking today."
🔑 The exact spot where it slips

Because the RESULT half looks like a Second Conditional ("would be", not "would have been"), it's easy to think the IF-half should match that and use a simple past too ("was not" instead of "had not been"). But the IF-half is still talking about something that really happened in the past — so it MUST keep the Third Conditional shape, "had (not) been ___", even though the result half looks like a Second. The giveaway that you need this Mixed recipe at all is a present-time word in the result — today, now, currently, still — signalling the result is happening RIGHT NOW, not back then.

Watch it worked out 👀

Sentence to complete: "If the school's irrigation system ___ properly maintained last year, the flower beds ___ so patchy right now."

Step 1 — spot the present-time word: "right now" is sitting in the result half. That's the signal: this needs the Mixed recipe.

Step 2 — fill the IF-half: the cause ("maintained the irrigation system") really happened (or didn't) LAST YEAR — that's the past, so it needs the Third-shape: "had not been" maintained. NOT "was not" — that's the exact trap.

Step 3 — fill the RESULT-half: the flower beds being patchy is happening RIGHT NOW — a present state, so it needs the Second-shape: "would not be" so patchy. NOT "would not have been" — that would push the result into the past too, which contradicts "right now".

Full answer: "If the school's irrigation system had not been properly maintained last year, the flower beds would not be so patchy right now."

The lesson: the RESULT half looking like a Second Conditional does NOT mean the IF-half gets to look like one too. Check each half against its OWN time — past cause, present result — separately.
