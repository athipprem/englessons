What is a "joining word"?

A joining word is a little word that links two ideas together, like glue. Look:

"It was raining. So we stayed inside."
"It was raining, but we went out anyway."
The words so and but join the two ideas — and they change the meaning. Cloze is just choosing the right joining word for each gap.

There are only a few jobs these words do, and a simple map to pick the right one. Let's meet the map. Next →

Today we master STEP 1 first
Step 1 is the foundation. If you always get "Starter or Joiner" right, the rest is easy. So the next screen practises ONLY Step 1, lots of times, until it feels automatic.

Drill 1 — Step 1 only: Starter or Joiner? ⚡

Look ONLY at the mark just before the gap. Full stop → Starter. Comma or middle of a sentence → Joiner. Nothing else to think about yet.

1. "The rain stopped. ____, we went outside."
2. "He was tired, ____ he kept going."
3. "She opened the door ____ the cat ran in."
4. "The bell rang. ____, the lesson ended."
5. "It was cold, ____ we wore our coats."
6. "The lights went off. ____, the room went dark."
7. "Dad cooked dinner ____ Mum washed up."
8. "We won the game. ____, we all celebrated."

Drill 2 — Now both steps 🌳

Step 1 (place) → Step 2 (job) → pick the word.

1. "The sun was very hot. ____, we found some shade." full stop
2. "The cake was small, ____ everyone got a piece." comma
3. "Tom drew the poster ____ Sam wrote the title." middle
4. "We practised hard. ____, we still lost." full stop
5. "She came first in the race. ____, she won the spelling prize." full stop

Drill 3 — Sunflower cloze 🌻

Walk the tree for each gap. Decide once, box it, move on.

Our class decided to grow sunflowers, (1)____ we each planted a seed in a cup. The cups sat on the windowsill (2)____ the sun warmed them each morning. Most seeds sprouted quickly. (3)____, one cup showed nothing at all. We watered it every day. (4)____, a tiny green shoot finally appeared. The shoot grew tall. (5)____, it opened into a bright yellow flower.

Gap 1 — "grow sunflowers, ____ we each planted a seed"
Gap 2 — "sat on the windowsill ____ the sun warmed them"
Gap 3 — "sprouted quickly. ____, one cup showed nothing"
Gap 4 — "watered it every day. ____, a tiny shoot appeared"
Gap 5 — "The shoot grew tall. ____, it opened into a flower"
Step 1: full stop = Starter, comma = Joiner.
Step 2: the job. Then box it and move on.
