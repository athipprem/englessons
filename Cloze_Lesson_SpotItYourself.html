🔎 An honest look at what went wrong last time

Last unit's practice had a hidden problem: every sentence was labelled “trigger word” or “no trigger word” BEFORE you even read it. That's on the lesson, not on you — it meant you never actually got to practise the real skill, spotting the trigger word with your own eyes. It's like practising penalty kicks with the goalkeeper already told which corner you're aiming for. Today there are NO labels. You have to find the trigger word yourself, every single time, exactly like the real test.

Quick recap — the rule itself 📢

You already understand this rule correctly — the problem was never the rule. Quick reminder before the real practice starts.

After recommend that / suggest that / insist that / require that / demand that — the verb goes back to its BASE FORM. No -s. No “to.” No tense change. Every time, no matter the subject.

The new routine — two steps, no clues 🕵️

From now on, every practice item works in two separate steps, and nothing tells you the answer to Step 1 in advance:

Read the WHOLE sentence carefully, start to finish.
STEP 1 — Decide: is there a trigger word (recommend/suggest/insist/require/demand) right before “that”? Click your answer.
STEP 2 — Only now do the verb-form choices appear. Pick the correct form based on your Step 1 decision.
🔑 Why no clues this time

The real test never tells you in advance whether a gap has a trigger word — you find out by reading carefully. Practising WITH that information removed is the only way to build the actual skill you'll need. It'll feel harder at first. That's the point — it means you're practising the real thing, not a shortcut version of it.
