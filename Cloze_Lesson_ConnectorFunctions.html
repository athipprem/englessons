Section 1

🔎 What Happened in Unit 2

In Unit 2, you got Q4, Q5, and Q6 wrong in the cloze section. But here is the interesting thing: you used all the correct words — you just put them in the wrong gaps.

The correct answers were: Q4 = however, Q5 = furthermore, Q6 = therefore. Your answers were: Q4 = therefore, Q5 = although, Q6 = furthermore.

The Root Cause

You were matching words from memory — "I know this word, it sounds right here" — instead of first asking: what job does this gap need? When you match by feel rather than by function, you rotate the words. All three of those words are connectors, so they all feel correct. The test is whether they do the right job.

The Fix

Cover the word bank. Read the sentence before and after the gap. Decide what the relationship is — CONTRAST, RESULT, ADDITION, or TIME — before you look at any word. Then choose the word that does that job.

Section 2

📄 The Four Jobs a Connector Can Do

Every connector in a cloze passage does one of four jobs. Learning the job first is the key skill.

Job What it means Quick substitution test Example words
CONTRAST Idea B is surprising or opposite given idea A Can you replace it with "but"? → CONTRAST however, although, yet, despite, on the other hand, nevertheless
RESULT Idea B follows from / is caused by idea A Can you replace it with "so"? → RESULT therefore, consequently, as a result, thus, hence
ADDITION Idea B adds more of the same kind as idea A Can you replace it with "and also"? → ADDITION furthermore, moreover, in addition, additionally, besides
TIME Idea B happens at a different point in time Does it show when or what order? → TIME meanwhile, eventually, subsequently, afterwards, finally
The Three-Second Test

Before touching the word bank: replace the gap with "but." → Works? CONTRAST. Replace with "so." → Works? RESULT. Replace with "and also." → Works? ADDITION. Replace with "then/during that time." → Works? TIME. This takes three seconds and gives you the job before you pick the word.

Section 3

💡 Reading the Relationship: Same Direction or Opposite?

The fastest way to identify the job is to read the sentence before the gap and the sentence after it — then ask one question:

1
Are both ideas going in the same direction? (both positive, both negative, or one building on the other) → RESULT or ADDITION
2
If same direction — does A cause B? (A happened → B happened because of it) → RESULT. If A and B are just both true with no cause-effect link → ADDITION
3
Are the ideas going in opposite directions? (A is positive, B is negative — or B surprises you after A) → CONTRAST
4
Is the gap marking when something happened? (before/after/during/at the same time as another event) → TIME

A quick way to tell RESULT from ADDITION

This is the trickiest pair because both go in the same direction.

RESULT — Ask: "Did A cause B?" If yes, use a result connector. It rained heavily, so the match was cancelled. The rain caused the cancellation.

ADDITION — Ask: "Is B just more of the same kind of idea as A, with no cause-effect?" If yes, use an addition connector. She speaks French. Furthermore, she speaks Mandarin. One doesn't cause the other — it's just more information in the same direction.

Section 4

📝 Worked Examples — Function First

Watch the four-step method applied to real gap sentences.

Example 1
"The new park was beautiful. _____, there were not enough benches for visitors."
1
Read both ideas: Park is beautiful. Not enough benches.
2
Replace with "but": "The park was beautiful, but there were not enough benches." ✓ Makes sense.
3
Job identified: CONTRAST — beautiful (positive) + not enough benches (negative). Opposite directions.
4
Choose from word bank: pick a CONTRAST word. "However" or "Nevertheless" both work here.
✓ Correct job: CONTRAST → use "However" or "Nevertheless"
Example 2
"The factory released toxic chemicals into the river. _____, the fish population in the area collapsed."
1
Read both ideas: Factory released chemicals. Fish population collapsed.
2
Replace with "so": "The factory released chemicals, so the fish collapsed." ✓ Makes sense.
3
Job: RESULT — the chemicals caused the fish to collapse. Cause → Effect.
4
Choose from word bank: "Consequently" or "As a result" or "Therefore" — all RESULT words.
✓ Correct job: RESULT → use "Consequently" / "As a result" / "Therefore"
Example 3
"The school introduced a new science programme. _____, it launched a drama club for students interested in performance."
1
Read both ideas: New science programme. Drama club launched.
2
Replace with "and also": "introduced a science programme, and also launched a drama club." ✓ Works.
3
Check for cause-effect: Did the science programme cause the drama club? No — they're just two separate new things.
4
Job: ADDITION — both are new initiatives; no cause-effect link, just adding more information.
✓ Correct job: ADDITION → use "Furthermore" / "In addition" / "Moreover"

Summary

✅ Key Takeaways

The Four Jobs

  • CONTRAST → ideas go opposite directions ("but")
  • RESULT → A causes B ("so")
  • ADDITION → both ideas same direction, no cause ("and also")
  • TIME → marks when or in what order ("then / during that time")

The Method

  • Cover the word bank first
  • Read the two ideas around the gap
  • Use the substitution test (but / so / and also)
  • Identify the job — then pick the matching word

RESULT vs ADDITION

  • RESULT: A causes B (cause → effect)
  • ADDITION: A and B are just both true
  • Ask: "Did A make B happen?" → Yes = RESULT
  • No cause-effect link → ADDITION

Common Traps

  • Don't match words by how they "feel"
  • Promising result + caution = CONTRAST (not RESULT)
  • Two separate facts = ADDITION (not RESULT)
  • Always apply the substitution test first
