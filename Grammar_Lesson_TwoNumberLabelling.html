You were asked to find the sentence with an error. You chose:

But this sentence is CORRECT. Why?

What Milestone Q10 and Q14 teach us

Both times, you saw "had + a noun" and your alarm bell rang — "suspicious!" — so you marked it as the error. But "had + a noun" is never wrong on its own. It just means the if-clause is a Second. Whether the sentence is right depends on the other half.

One half is never the error.
The error lives in the MATCH between the two halves.

The Two-Number Habit ✏️

Here is the whole trick. Before you choose an answer, write a tiny number above each half of every sentence. Two numbers. Every time. No exceptions.

What you seeWriteWhy
had + a noun / adjective (a bigger gym, more space, taller trees)2simple past of "have" → Second if-clause
had + a past participle verb (had been fixed, had left, had arrived)3past perfect → Third if-clause
would + base verb (would host, would visit, would dock)2Second result
would have + verb (would have hosted, would have stayed)3Third result
Then read your two numbers like a traffic light

2 | 2 → green. Match. Correct sentence — move on.
3 | 3 → green. Match. Correct sentence — move on.
2 | 3 → red. MISMATCH. This is the error.

The trap that catches you
A sentence with "had + a noun" looks odd, but if its result is would + base verb, the numbers read 2|2 — a perfectly correct Second Conditional. Don't blame it. Find the one whose numbers disagree.
Worked example

"If the orchard had taller trees, the birds would have nested higher."
Half 1: "had taller trees" → had + noun → 2
Half 2: "would have nested" → would have + verb → 3
Read it: 2 | 3 → MISMATCH. Found it — and no correct sentence got the blame.

Drill 1 — Two numbers, then decide 🔢

For each sentence, label both halves in your head, then choose what the two numbers say. Take your time on the first few, then speed up.

1. "If the café had a sunnier terrace, more customers would linger after lunch."
2. "If the rope had been checked, the climbers would have noticed the fray."
3. "If the orchard had taller trees, the birds would have nested higher."
4. "If the runner had paced herself, she would have finished the marathon."
5. "If the hall had brighter lighting, the play would look more dramatic." (careful — "had + noun" alarm bell!)
6. "If the dam had been inspected, the cracks would have been spotted."
7. "If the team had a taller centre, they would have dominated the rebounds."
8. "If the bakery had cheaper flour, it would lower its prices."
