Contrast Identification

Structure is solved. Function is the only remaining gap — and CONTRAST is the hardest function to spot.
🎯 ISA Priority — Cloze Test
Section 1 of 5

🔎 The Problem With CONTRAST

You now get the structural type right every time — full stop means sentence-starter, no full stop means clause-joiner. That was a big step. The remaining errors are all about function: specifically, when to choose CONTRAST instead of ADDITION.

Here is why CONTRAST is the trickiest function to spot: ADDITION and RESULT both have a clear logical test (Because Backwards works for RESULT; same-direction extra information works for ADDITION). But CONTRAST does not feel like addition OR result — it feels like a surprise. And surprises are harder to notice when you are under pressure.

The Trap

When the Because Backwards test says NO (= not RESULT), students often default to ADDITION. But sometimes the answer is CONTRAST — and CONTRAST has its own test. You cannot skip it.

Section 2 of 5

😲 The Surprise Test — Your New Step 2b

CONTRAST connects two ideas where the second idea is unexpected or surprising given the first. Think of it this way: if someone read the first idea and predicted what would come next, CONTRAST means their prediction was wrong.

😲 The Surprise Test — Ask This Question
"She trained every day for a month."
Question: What would you expect to come next?
Expected: "She won the race." / "She performed well."
"She trained every day for a month. _______, she came last in the race."
Is coming last surprising given the training? → YES
✓ CONTRAST → However, / Although / Nevertheless
"She trained every day for a month. _______, she also improved her diet."
Is improving her diet surprising given the training? → NO (same direction)
✓ ADDITION → Furthermore, / Moreover, / In addition
The Key Question for CONTRAST

"Given the first idea — is the second idea surprising or unexpected?" YES → CONTRAST. NO → go to ADDITION.

📈 The Complete Three-Way Function Test

Here is the full decision process for every cloze gap — three questions, in order. Never skip a step.

📈 Three-Way Function Test — Use This Every Time
Step 1 — Because Backwards
"Second idea happened BECAUSE of the first idea?"
YES ✓
RESULT
consequently, therefore, as a result, so
NO → go to Step 2
Not RESULT
Step 2 — Surprise Test
"Given the first idea — is the second idea surprising or unexpected?"
YES — surprising 😲
CONTRAST

💡 Memory: Result → Contrast → Addition. Test them in that order. Never jump straight to Addition.

✀️ Cross-Off Rule — Compulsory Final Step
1
Every time you write a letter in an answer box, put a small cross next to that letter in the word bank. Mark it as used.
2
When all 7 gaps are filled, look at the word bank. One letter will have no cross — that is the distractor.
3
Danger signal: if two letters in the same function family are in the bank (e.g. two CONTRAST words: however + nevertheless), one of them MUST be the distractor. Once you use one, the other is automatically eliminated.
4
If you have used any letter twice — stop and go back. You made an error. Every letter is used at most once.
