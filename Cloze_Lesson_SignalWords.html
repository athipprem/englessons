Signal Words & Connectors

How to tell CONTRAST from RESULT from ADDITION — and never mix them up again.
⚠ Unit 1 Gap — Q5/Q6 connector swap · Targeted fix
Section 1

🔗 Why Connectors Are the Key to Cloze

In Unit 1, you got Q5 and Q6 wrong — not because you didn't know the words, but because you put however and consequently in the wrong gaps. You knew both words. You just confused which idea called for which connector.

Connectors (also called signal words or linking words) are the glue of a passage. They tell the reader: "Hold on, something is about to change" or "Here comes the result" or "There's more of the same idea."

The Golden Rule

Before choosing a connector, ask: what is the relationship between the two ideas? Contrast? Result? Addition? Time? The relationship tells you the connector — not the other way around.

Section 2

📄 The Four Connector Types

There are four main types of signal words you will see in cloze passages. Learn to spot which type you need before looking at the word bank.

Type What it signals Key words Quick test
CONTRAST Ideas that are opposite or surprising given what came before however, although, yet, even though, despite, on the other hand, nevertheless Could you say "but" here? → CONTRAST
RESULT The second idea is caused by / follows from the first consequently, therefore, as a result, thus, so, hence Could you say "so" here? → RESULT
ADDITION Adding more of the same kind of idea furthermore, moreover, in addition, also, besides, additionally Could you say "and also" here? → ADDITION
TIME Showing when or in what order something happened eventually, meanwhile, subsequently, once, finally, afterwards Is one event before or after another? → TIME
The Trap in Unit 1

"However" and "consequently" look similar in length and both feel formal — but they are complete opposites in meaning. "However" says CONTRAST (something unexpected follows). "Consequently" says RESULT (something expected follows because of what came before).

Section 3

🔍 How to Identify the Type from the Passage

Here is the two-sentence method. Read the sentence before the gap and the sentence after it. Then ask: are the two ideas going in the same direction or opposite directions?

Same direction → RESULT or ADDITION

If idea A causes or leads to idea B, use a RESULT connector. If idea B just adds more of idea A, use an ADDITION connector.

Opposite direction → CONTRAST

If idea B surprises you given idea A, or they pull in opposite directions, use a CONTRAST connector.

Shortcut: the Substitution Test

Replace the gap with "but." Does the sentence make sense? If yes → CONTRAST. Now replace it with "so." Does it make sense? If yes → RESULT. Replace with "and also" → ADDITION. This takes 5 seconds and works every time.

Section 4

✍️ Worked Examples

Example 1 — "The team trained hard every day. __________, they lost the final match."
1
Read both ideas: trained hard vs lost the final.
2
Are they going the same direction? Training hard → winning seems expected. Losing is a SURPRISE.
3
Substitution test: "But, they lost." ✓ Makes sense → this is a CONTRAST.
4
Choose a CONTRAST connector: However, they lost the final match.
✓ Answer: However  |  CONTRAST
Example 2 — "The water pipes burst during the storm. __________, the entire ground floor was flooded."
1
Read both ideas: pipes burst → floor flooded.
2
Same direction? Yes — burst pipes causing flooding is expected. No surprise.
3
Substitution test: "So, the entire ground floor was flooded." ✓ Makes sense → RESULT.
4
Choose a RESULT connector: Consequently, the entire ground floor was flooded.
✓ Answer: Consequently  |  RESULT
Example 3 — "The museum has paintings from over fifty countries. __________, it houses one of the world's largest sculpture collections."
1
Read both ideas: paintings from 50 countries + large sculpture collection.
2
Both ideas describe impressive things in the museum — same direction, more of the same.
3
Substitution test: "And also, it houses…" ✓ Makes sense → ADDITION.
4
Choose an ADDITION connector: Furthermore, it houses one of the world's largest sculpture collections.
✓ Answer: Furthermore  |  ADDITION

Summary

✅ What to Remember

The Four Types

  • CONTRAST — however, although, yet, despite
  • RESULT — consequently, therefore, as a result
  • ADDITION — furthermore, moreover, in addition
  • TIME — eventually, meanwhile, subsequently

The Method

  • Read both sentences first
  • Ask: same direction or opposite?
  • Use the substitution test (but / so / and also)
  • Choose the right type, then pick the word
