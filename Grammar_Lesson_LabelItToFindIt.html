Why this matters 🔍

One specific type of question — "which of these four sentences has the error?" — has been missed on every single attempt for three tests running. That's unusual: normally a couple of explanations and some practice fixes a skill. When something this specific keeps slipping, it usually means the FORMAT needs to change, not the explanation. Today we try something different: instead of just thinking through the steps in your head, you'll physically LABEL every option on screen before choosing — the same trick that fixed stuck skills before.

A different way in 📝

Explaining the steps out loud hasn't been enough for this one — three tests in a row, still stuck. So today isn't another explanation. It's a physical routine: for every single option, you will click to LABEL its two halves before you're allowed to pick an answer. No skipping, no guessing by feel.

For each option: label the IF-half (THIRD-shape "had ___ed" or SECOND-shape plain past), then label the RESULT-half ("would have ___ed" = THIRD, plain "would ___" = SECOND). If the two labels don't match, that option is broken.
🔑 Why labelling works when explaining doesn't

This is the exact same trick that fixed the cloze pencil-routine and the very first grammar cross-check, years ago in this programme — writing a label forces your eyes to actually stop and look at each piece, instead of your brain jumping to a "feels right" guess. Today you'll do it eight times per question (2 labels × 4 options) before choosing.

Watch it labelled, step by step 👀

Option 1: "If the kettle had boiled dry, the safety switch would have clicked off." IF-half label: THIRD ("had boiled"). RESULT-half label: THIRD ("would have clicked"). Labels MATCH → clean.

Option 2: "If the battery had drained, the alarm would stop working." IF-half label: THIRD ("had drained"). RESULT-half label: SECOND (plain "would stop"). Labels DON'T MATCH → this is the error.

Option 3: "If the wiring had frayed, the lamp would have short-circuited." IF-half: THIRD. RESULT-half: THIRD. MATCH → clean.

Option 4: "If the fuse had blown, the power would have cut out instantly." IF-half: THIRD. RESULT-half: THIRD. MATCH → clean.

Answer: Option 2 — the only one where the two labels didn't match. Notice: every single option got labelled, even the ones that turned out clean. That's the whole point — no skipping ahead.
