You beat the cloze monster. 🏆

"Although" at a comma, the "despite" trap, the cause-and-effect words — all correct at Milestone 1. Only one corner is left: telling apart two words that mean the same thing but live at different levels.

Before We Start — the two-step check 🔍

Warm-up 1 — Level (Step 1)

"The bus was late. ____, we still arrived on time." — Is the gap a sentence-starter or a clause-joiner?

Warm-up 2 — Function (Step 2)

"The rain finally stopped, ____ the match could begin." — Which job does the gap do?

Warm-up 3 — the one that got away (Milestone 1)

"Mia finished her test early. ____, the others were still writing." — meanwhile or while?

What happened at the Milestone

Three marks slipped on the same idea: meanwhile / while and furthermore got shuffled between gaps. You knew the words — you just hadn't read the punctuation to decide which level each gap needed. That is exactly what this lesson fixes.

Same job, two levels 🪜

Some jobs have two words — one for the start of a new sentence, one for the middle of a sentence. They mean the same thing. The punctuation just before the gap tells you which one to use.

sentence-starter		After a comma / mid-sentence

The one move
Look at the mark just before the gap. Full stop → take the left column. Comma or mid-sentence → take the right column. Decide the meaning first, then let the punctuation pick the level.
Two gaps, same meaning, different word

"Sam read his book. Meanwhile, Ana built a tower." → full stop → starter → meanwhile.
"Sam read his book while Ana built a tower." → no full stop → joiner → while.
Same meaning (at the same time). The full stop is the only thing that changed.

Drill 1 — Starter or Joiner? ⚡

Only look at the punctuation just before the gap. Full stop = Starter. Comma or mid-sentence = Joiner.

1. "…the storm passed. ____, the festival reopened."
2. "…she trained hard, ____ she still felt nervous."
3. "…the shop closed early ____ the staff were unwell."
4. "…we missed the last bus. ____, we walked home."
5. "…he packed extra snacks, ____ the journey was long."
6. "…the lights went out. ____, the show carried on."
7. "…the dog began to bark ____ the postman walked up the path."
8. "…it rained all afternoon. ____, the picnic was cancelled."

Drill 2 — Pick the level-twin 👯

Same meaning, two words. The punctuation decides. Choose the right one.

1. "Sam read quietly. ____, Ana built a tower." full stop
2. "Sam read quietly ____ Ana built a tower." no full stop
3. "The bridge was shut, ____ traffic crawled for miles." comma
4. "The bridge was shut. ____, traffic crawled for miles." full stop
5. "The film was long, ____ nobody left early." comma
6. "The film was long. ____, nobody left early." full stop

Drill 3 — Park Clean-Up cloze 🌳

Five gaps. For each one, read the punctuation, decide the meaning, then pick the word at the right level. Each gap pits the right word against its level-twin.

The whole street turned out for the park clean-up. Mr Patel raked the leaves (1)____ his daughter filled the bins. The slide had been covered in thick mud. (2)____, a team scrubbed it until it shone. By lunchtime the playground looked brand new. (3)____, the flower beds were still a tangle of weeds. The midday sun grew hot, (4)____ everyone took a break in the shade. They worked on without a single complaint. (5)____, across town another group was clearing the riverbank.

Gap 1 — "raked the leaves ____ his daughter filled the bins"
Gap 2 — "covered in thick mud. ____, a team scrubbed it"
Gap 3 — "looked brand new. ____, the flower beds were still weeds"
Gap 4 — "the sun grew hot, ____ everyone took a break"
Gap 5 — "without a single complaint. ____, across town another group…"
