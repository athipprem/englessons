Why this matters 🌍

Joining words are the signposts of good English. They tell the reader how two ideas connect — caused it, surprised it, happened at the same time, or added to it. You use them every day ("I was tired, so I went to bed"; "it rained, but we still played"). Getting them right makes your writing sound clear and grown-up — and cloze questions are worth easy marks in every test once the routine is automatic.

The tricky twins: however vs although

Both mean contrast (a surprise). They have the SAME job — so Step 2 can't separate them. Only Step 1 (the level) tells them apart:

however = sentence-starter

Comes after a full stop, then a comma.
"It rained hard. However, we played on."

although = clause-joiner

Sits inside one sentence (a comma, no full stop).
"It rained hard, although we played on."

The whole trick
Same meaning, different level. Full stop before the gap → however. Comma / middle → although. Do Step 1 first and the twins stop being tricky.

Drill 1 — however or although? ⚡

Both are contrast. Just check Step 1: full stop = however, comma/middle = although.

1. "It was raining hard. ____, the match went ahead."
2. "It was raining hard, ____ the match went ahead."
3. "The shop looked closed, ____ the door was actually open."
4. "The shop looked closed. ____, the door was actually open."
5. "Ben felt nervous, ____ he walked onto the stage."
6. "Ben felt nervous. ____, he walked onto the stage."

Drill 2 — the book fair cloze 📚

Walk the whole tree for each gap. Decide once, box it, move on.

Our class ran a book fair, (1)____ we could raise money for new library books. We set up the stalls early (2)____ the first customers arrived. Most books sold quickly. (3)____, a few were left at the end. We counted the coins carefully. (4)____, we had made over two thousand baht. The library ordered ten new titles. (5)____, it bought a comfy reading beanbag.

Gap 1 — "ran a book fair, ____ we could raise money"
Gap 2 — "set up the stalls early ____ the first customers arrived"
Gap 3 — "sold quickly. ____, a few were left at the end"
Gap 4 — "counted the coins carefully. ____, we had made over two thousand baht"
Gap 5 — "ordered ten new titles. ____, it bought a comfy beanbag"
Step 1 first — full stop = starter, comma = joiner.
That alone settles however vs although. Then box it and move on.
