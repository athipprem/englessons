Two families, same shapes 🏠

"So" and "however" can both be comma, mid-sentence words. "As a result" and "however" can both be full-stop, sentence-starter words. The SHAPE doesn't tell them apart — you have to check the MEANING too. There are two families to know:

RESULT family
The second thing happened BECAUSE of the first thing. One thing caused the other.

Members: so (mid-sentence) · as a result (sentence-start)

"It rained all night, so the field flooded." (the rain CAUSED the flood)
CONTRAST family
The second thing is a SURPRISE — the opposite of what you'd expect, given the first thing.

Members: although (mid-sentence) · however (sentence-start)

"It rained all night, although the field stayed dry." (staying dry is a SURPRISE after all that rain)
🔑 The two-step check, every time

Step 1 — SHAPE: full stop before the gap = sentence-starter; comma/mid-sentence = clause-joiner. Step 2 — FAMILY: does the second part happen BECAUSE of the first (→ RESULT) or does it SURPRISE you, the opposite of what you'd expect (→ CONTRAST)? Only after BOTH checks do you know the exact word.
