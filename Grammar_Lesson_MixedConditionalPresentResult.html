Unit 5  ·  Grammar

Mixed Conditional: Present Result

One tiny change separates Third from Mixed Conditional. Here is exactly what it is — and two ways to never get it wrong again.
🎯 ISA Priority — Grammar
Section 1 of 6

🔄 Quick Review: Third Conditional

You already know this one well. Here is a fast reminder before the new lesson.

The Third Conditional imagines a past event that did NOT happen — and shows the past result that would have followed.

⏲ Third Conditional
If + had + past participle,   would have + past participle
"If she had studied harder, she would have passed the exam."
She did NOT study → she did NOT pass. Both in the PAST.
PAST result — finished, done
⚡ Mixed Conditional — NEW
If + had + past participle,   would + BASE VERB
"If she had studied harder, she would be a doctor now."
She did NOT study → she is NOT a doctor. Condition PAST, result NOW.
PRESENT result — still true now

See the difference? The if-clause is identical in both. The only change is in the result clause.

Section 2 of 6

💡 The One Change That Matters

Look at these two sentences. The if-clause is the same. Only the result clause changes.

⏲ Third Conditional — past result
"If the builders had finished on time, the hotel would have opened in July."
The opening in July was a past event. It is done. Over. → would have + past participle
⚡ Mixed Conditional — present result
"If the builders had finished on time, the hotel would be open today."
The hotel being open is a current, ongoing situation. → would + base verb (no "have")
The Exact Error to Avoid

Third: would HAVE been (past, finished)  →  "the hotel would have opened."
Mixed: would BE (present, ongoing)  →  "the hotel would be open today."
The word "have" disappears in the Mixed Conditional result clause.

The Quick Check

Before answering any conditional question: scan the result clause for a present time signal. Found one? → Mixed (would + base verb). No time signal? → probably Third (would have + past participle).

Section 4 of 6

✂️ The "Delete HAVE" Rule — Mechanical Backup

If you have identified a Mixed Conditional but are worried about getting the result clause wrong, use this three-step mechanical rule. It is almost impossible to get wrong.

✂️ Delete HAVE — 3 Steps
1
You see a time signal like today or now → you know it is Mixed Conditional.
2
Start with the Third Conditional result clause you already know: "would have been" → delete the word "have".
3
Fix the remaining verb to base form: "would be". Done. That is your Mixed Conditional result.

Example: you want to say "the bridge would have been safe". You see "today" → Mixed. Delete "have" → "would been" → fix → "would be". The bridge would be safe today.

Why This Works

The only difference between Third and Mixed result clauses is the word "have". Third = would have done. Mixed = would do. Deleting "have" and fixing the verb is the fastest and most reliable mechanical path.

Section 5 of 6

📝 Worked Examples

Example 1 — Third or Mixed? (easy)
"If the captain had checked the weather forecast, the ship _______ into the storm."
1
Scan for time signal in result clause: none. No "now", "today", "still".
2
The result (sailing into the storm) is a past event — it happened at the same time as the voyage. → Third Conditional.
3
Use: would have + past participle → "would not have sailed"
✓ Answer: "…the ship would not have sailed into the storm."
Example 2 — Mixed with "today" signal
"If the ancient city walls _______ preserved, they _______ a major tourist attraction today."
1
Scan for time signal: "today" → present result → Mixed Conditional.
2
If-clause: past event that did not happen → "had been preserved".
3
Delete HAVE: "would have been" → "would be". The walls being an attraction is a present state.
✓ Answer: "If they had been preserved, they would be a major tourist attraction today."
Example 3 — Error-spotting (find the mistake)
Which is WRONG?   A: "If he had won the prize, he would have been famous."   B: "If he had won the prize, he would have been famous today."
1
Sentence A: "If he had won… he would have been famous." — no time signal → Third Conditional. Past result. ✓ Correct.
2
Sentence B: "…would have been famous today." — "today" is a present signal → Mixed Conditional → must use "would be", NOT "would have been".
3
Sentence B is wrong. The correct version: "If he had won the prize, he would be famous today."
✓ Sentence B contains the error. "today" = present signal → result must be "would be", not "would have been".

Third Conditional

  • If + had + past participle
  • would HAVE + past participle
  • Result is in the PAST — no time signal

Mixed Conditional

  • If + had + past participle (same)
  • would + BASE VERB (no "have")
  • Result is NOW — time signals: now, today, still

Your Two Tools

  • Timeline test: find "now/today/still" → result is present → Mixed → would + base verb
  • Delete HAVE: start with "would have been" → delete "have" → fix → "would be"
