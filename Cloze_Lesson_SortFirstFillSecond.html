Unit 6  ·  Cloze

Sort First, Fill Second

The reason the same mistake keeps happening — and one step that stops it before it starts.
🎯 ISA Priority — Cloze Test
Section 1 of 6

🤔 Why the Same Error Keeps Coming Back

You have learned the connector families. You have learned the Because Backwards test. You know the difference between ADDITION and RESULT. But the same mistake still appears in tests — choosing however when you need although, or picking although when you need furthermore.

Here is exactly why this keeps happening: you are looking at the gap and choosing a word at the same time. That is one step too many to do at once. You are trying to think about the full stop, the function, and the word all together — and under pressure, one of them gets skipped.

The Root Cause

When you look at the word bank and pick a word in one go, you are making three decisions simultaneously: structural type, function, and word choice. That is too much. This lesson splits it into two separate jobs done at two separate times.

The fix is simple: do not touch the passage until you have sorted the word bank first.

Section 2 of 6

📜 Step 0 — Sort the Word Bank Into Two Bins

Before you read the passage, before you look at any gap — sort every word in the word bank into one of two groups. This takes about 30 seconds and it makes everything else automatic.

📜 The Two Bins — Sort Before You Start
● Bin 1 — Sentence-Starters
Come after a full stop (.) and begin a brand new sentence. Always followed by a comma. The two sentences they connect can stand alone.
However, Furthermore, Consequently, Moreover, Therefore, Nevertheless, Meanwhile, In addition, As a result,
● Bin 2 — Clause-Joiners
Live inside a sentence — no full stop before them. The part of the sentence they introduce cannot stand alone. Usually no comma directly after them.
although because while whereas since even though unless
💡 Once you know which bin each word belongs to, the Period Test at each gap becomes a one-second job: full stop? → grab from Bin 1. No full stop? → grab from Bin 2. You have already done the hard thinking.
💡 Quick Memory Test

Sentence-starters are always capitalised in the word bank and followed by a comma (e.g. However,). Clause-joiners are lower case and have no comma after them (e.g. although). One look at the capitalisation tells you the bin.

Section 3 of 6

📄 The Complete 3-Step Process

Here is the full method. Step 0 is new — it comes before everything else and it is the most important step.

📜 Step 0 — Sort
Before reading the passage, sort all word bank words into Bin 1 (sentence-starters) and Bin 2 (clause-joiners). Write S or J next to each word if that helps.
🔎 Step 1 — Period Test
At each gap: is there a full stop before it? YES → grab from Bin 1 (sentence-starters). NO → grab from Bin 2 (clause-joiners).
💥 Step 2 — Function Test
From the correct bin, which word fits? CONTRAST / RESULT / ADDITION / TIME? Use the Because Backwards test to decide between ADDITION and RESULT.
Section 4 of 6

✂️ The Distractor Rule — Cross It Off

Every cloze word bank has one word that is NOT needed. It is called the distractor. The distractor is always a real connector word — it is just not used in this particular passage.

Here is how the distractor catches students: when two words from the same function family are in the bank (for example, both therefore and as a result are RESULT connectors), students sometimes grab the wrong one. One is the answer. The other is the distractor.

✂️ The Cross-Off Rule — Do This Every Time
1
As you choose each letter, cross it off the word bank (or circle it, or tick it — whatever you prefer). Mark it as used.
2
When you have filled all 7 gaps, one letter will have no cross. That is the distractor.
3
If you used the same letter twice — stop. Go back. You made an error somewhere. The distractor rule means every letter is used at most once.
The Distractor Is Never a Trick

The unused word is always a valid connector — it just does not fit any gap in this passage. If you have crossed off all letters correctly and one remains, that is the right answer. Do not second-guess it.

📝 Full Worked Example — Sort, Then Fill

Word bank:  A. furthermore  B. however  C. consequently  D. although  E. while  F. therefore  (distractor)

Step 0 first — sort the bank before reading anything.

LetterWordBinFunction
Afurthermore,Sentence-StarterADDITION
Bhowever,Sentence-StarterCONTRAST
Cconsequently,Sentence-StarterRESULT
DalthoughClause-JoinerCONTRAST
EwhileClause-JoinerTIME
Ftherefore,Sentence-StarterRESULT

Notice: A and F are both RESULT sentence-starters. Only one will be used. The other is the distractor. That is the trap to watch for.

Now fill the passage:

Jake loved playing football, D — although he had never played in an organised match before. He missed the first three training sessions. C — Consequently, the coach almost dropped him from the squad. Jake trained twice as hard to make up for it. A — Furthermore, he stayed an extra hour every Saturday to practise his shooting. E — while the rest of the team warmed up, Jake was already on the pitch. He improved faster than anyone expected. B — However, the final match was against the strongest team in the league.

The distractor was F (therefore) — it was not needed because C (consequently) already filled the RESULT slot. Cross-off check: A✓ B✓ C✓ D✓ E✓ F✗ — F is unused = distractor ✓

🎉 All 5 Complete!

You now have a three-step process. Use it every time — Sort first, then fill.

Step 0 — Sort the Bank

  • Capitalised + comma after = Sentence-Starter (Bin 1)
  • Lower case + no comma = Clause-Joiner (Bin 2)
  • Do this BEFORE reading any gap

Steps 1 & 2 — At Each Gap

  • Full stop before gap → grab from Bin 1
  • No full stop → grab from Bin 2
  • Then: ADDITION / RESULT / CONTRAST / TIME?

Distractor Cross-Off Rule

  • Cross off each letter as you use it
  • The one letter with no cross = the distractor
  • If you used any letter twice — go back, you made an error
