One mistake, three lost marks

Gap 1 needed although, but took however. That meant Gap 7 — which needed however — found the word already gone. You were pushed into the distractor. One structural slip became a three-mark chain reaction.

Why label ALL the gaps first?
Labelling every gap S or J in one calm sweep takes about 30 seconds — and it means every structural decision is finished before the word bank starts pulling at your attention. In Unit 9, the one structural error happened while juggling words and structure at the same time. The routine separates them.
✅ Which words are S and which are J?

S — sentence-starters: however · consequently · furthermore · meanwhile · moreover · as a result
J — clause-joiners: although · because · while · since
⛔ Trap: despite — a preposition. It can never introduce a full clause (subject + verb), so it can never fill a connector gap. If it's in the bank, it's almost always the distractor.

Head-to-Head: although vs while 🥊

Both are J words — clause-joiners. In Unit 9, Gap 5 needed while but got although. The level was right; the JOB was wrong. Here is how to tell them apart every time.

Two quick tests — run them in order
⏱ The Same-Time Test
Can you say "at the same time" between the two ideas?
YES → TIME → while
😲 The Surprise Test
Is it surprising that both ideas are true together?
YES → CONTRAST → although
Example pair — same shape, different job
"Leo whistled ___ he washed the car." → whistling and washing happen at the same time → TIME → while.
"Leo whistled cheerfully, ___ the car was covered in mud again by lunchtime." → cheerful whistling + ruined work = surprising together → CONTRAST → although.

Find the Twin 🕵️ — the rescue move

This is the move that saves you when the Unit 9 chain reaction starts. You reach the end, and the leftover word does not fit the last gap. Do not force it in. The leftover word is a clue: somewhere behind you, a gap is holding the wrong word.

Leftover word doesn't fit?
The mistake is BEHIND you. Check the leftover's job — then find the gap holding its twin.

Practise being the detective. In each round, a finished cloze has ONE wrong answer hiding in it. Use the leftover word's S/J label and job to track it down, then click the guilty gap.

Round 1 — The Art Fair
Rosa's painting won first prize, (1) however she had nearly thrown it away the week before. The judges loved the colours. (2) furthermore, they praised the unusual frame. Visitors queued all afternoon to see it (3) because the local paper had shown it on the front page.
Leftover word: although — a J word (CONTRAST). It fits no remaining gap. Which gap took its place?
Round 2 — The Bike Ride
Jon checked the tyres (1) while Priya filled the water bottles. The hill was far steeper than the map suggested. (2) consequently, everyone pushed their bikes to the top. They ate lunch by the lake, (3) meanwhile the clouds were already gathering over the hills.
Leftover word: although — a J word (CONTRAST). Which gap is breaking the rules?
In the real test
The leftover check costs you 20 seconds at the end of Section A. The Unit 9 chain reaction cost 3 marks. That is the best trade in the whole test.
