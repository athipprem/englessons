Why guessing fails 🚫

Q8 appeared in Unit 7 and Unit 8 — identical format, identical error. The Had Test is the fix. Once you own this check, Q8-type questions become automatic.

❌ Without the Had Test — Q8 in two consecutive tests:

You saw "had won" and chose it as Second Conditional. But "had won" = had + won (past participle verb) → past perfect → Third Conditional.
The Second Conditional option was B: "If Maya had a bigger bedroom..." — "had" + "a bigger bedroom" (noun) = simple past of "have" = Second.

The Had Test: ask one question — "Is the word after 'had' a past participle verb?"
YES (had won, had been, had started) → past perfect → Third or Mixed Conditional
NO (had a room, had more time, had better equipment) → simple past of "have" → Second Conditional

The Had Test — Make It a Reflex ⚡

For every if-clause containing "had", ask exactly one question: "Is the word immediately after 'had' a past participle verb?"

✅ Second Conditional
had + noun / adjective / adverb
"had a bigger room" → noun ✓
"had more funding" → noun ✓
"had better equipment" → adj ✓

Result: would + base verb
🔴 Third / Mixed Conditional
had + past participle verb
"had won" → won ✓
"had been built" → been ✓
"had started" → started ✓

Result: would have + past participle (Third)
or would + base verb + now/today (Mixed)
⚠ Common trap — "had not been"
"If the bridge had not been rebuilt..." → had + "been" → past participle verb → Third/Mixed. Do not be distracted by "not" — apply the test to the verb that follows "had".

Rapid-Fire Had Test Drill ⚡

Click 2nd or 3rd/Mix for each if-clause. Do this without pausing — the goal is to make the check automatic.

Will Ban 🚫

In the result clause of any conditional — Second, Third, or Mixed — the word "will" is banned. Always replace with "would".

Wrong ❌Correct ✅
"If it rained, the match will be cancelled.""If it rained, the match would be cancelled."
"If she had trained harder, she will have won.""If she had trained harder, she would have won."
"If they had left earlier, they will be there now.""If they had left earlier, they would be there now."
Scan every result clause. See "will"? Cross it out. Write "would".
This check takes two seconds. Apply it before reading the options — not after.

Mismatch Check — Second + Third ❌

If the if-clause is Second Conditional but the result clause is Third Conditional (or vice versa) — that is a grammar error. You must spot this in error-detection questions.

❌ Mismatch example (from Unit 7 Q12):

"If the director had more creative control [Second if-clause], she would have produced a very different film [Third result]."
→ Second + Third = MISMATCH ERROR.

Mixed Conditional — If-clause Rule 🗓

A Mixed Conditional describes a past event whose effect is still felt now. The time signal "now / today / still" in the result clause is the key clue.

Past event → always past perfect in the if-clause
"If the ancient oak had not been cut down fifty years ago, the park would feel different today."

"Fifty years ago" = past event → if-clause must use past perfect (had + pp), not simple past.
"Today" in the result → would + base verb (not "would have").
✅ Mixed Conditional formula:

If + had + past participle [past event], … would + base verb + now/today/still [present result]
