Quick reminder — the routine that keeps working 🔧

No new teaching today for error-scan — just a reminder of exactly what's been working for two tests running.

For every option: write "3rd" above the IF-half if it says "had ___ed"; write "2nd" if it's a plain past. Write "3rd" above the RESULT-half if it says "would have ___ed"; write "2nd" if it's a plain "would ___". If the two labels for one option don't match, that's the error.

Fixing one thing — "regret" in a live announcement 🎙️

Last test's one grammar miss: a "regret" sentence that was a LIVE announcement, not a look-back memory. Here's the two situations side by side, so the difference is crystal clear.

📷 Photo-Album Rosa — looking back

Something already happened, long ago, and you feel sorry about it now, looking back. Use -ing.

"Years later, the retired captain regretted missing the final match of his career."

🎙️ Announcement Rosa — happening right now

Someone is formally telling people something, live, in this moment — often at a podium, on a stage, or in a notice. Use to + verb.

"The manager stood at the podium and announced, 'We regret to delay tonight's launch due to the storm.'"

The tell: is someone standing up right now, formally telling people something (Announcement Rosa, use "to")? Or is someone looking back at something long over, feeling sorry (Photo-Album Rosa, use "-ing")?

"The captain gathered the team and said, 'I regret ___ (tell) you all that the final has been postponed.'" Which form?
"Looking back on that summer, she always regretted ___ (not/practise) harder before the tryouts." Which form?
