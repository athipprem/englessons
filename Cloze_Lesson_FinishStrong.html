Why this matters 🌍

In a cloze passage, every gap is worth the same mark — so the last gap counts exactly as much as the first. The easiest marks to lose are the ones at the end, when you're tired or rushing to finish. Slowing down for the final gaps is one of the simplest ways to score higher.

What happened last time 🔎

Your although vs however was spot on — that twin pair is fixed. The three you missed were all at the very end of the passage: so, meanwhile, moreover. The first four were right, the last three slipped — that's a finishing habit, not a knowledge problem. Today we sharpen those three sentence-starters and build a "finish strong" habit. Next →

All three come after a full stop. The only question is the job. Here's how to tell them apart:

as a result 🎯

The first sentence caused the second.
"It poured with rain. As a result, the game stopped."

meanwhile ⏱️

Two things at the same time (often in two places).
"The boys cooked. Meanwhile, the girls set the table."

moreover ➕

An extra point added on top.
"She won the race. Moreover, she broke the record."

Quick test
Ask: did the first thing make the second happen? → as a result. Are they happening together? → meanwhile. Is it just one more point? → moreover.

Drill 1 — as a result / meanwhile / moreover ⚡

All sentence-starters. Pick the job: caused → as a result, same time → meanwhile, extra → moreover.

1. "A tree had fallen across the trail. ____, the hikers had to turn back."
2. "Her cake won first prize. ____, it sold out within minutes."
3. "The runners stretched on the track. ____, the coaches set up the hurdles."
4. "The power suddenly cut out. ____, the whole hall went dark."
5. "He came top in maths. ____, he was chosen as class captain."
6. "The band tuned their instruments. ____, the crowd found their seats."

Drill 2 — the bake-sale cloze 🧁

Walk the tree for each gap. The last two gaps are ringed in gold — give them the same care as the first.

Our class held a bake sale, (1)____ we could raise money for the trip. Parents baked all morning (2)____ the children made the price labels. The cupcakes sold out fast. (3)____, the brownies were left untouched at first. We lowered the price. (4)____, the brownies vanished too. (5)____, we raised more money than any class before us.

Gap 1 — "held a bake sale, ____ we could raise money"
Gap 2 — "baked all morning ____ the children made the labels"
Gap 3 — "cupcakes sold out fast. ____, the brownies were left untouched"
⭐ Gap 4 — "We lowered the price. ____, the brownies vanished too"
⭐ Gap 5 — "____, we raised more money than any class before us"
