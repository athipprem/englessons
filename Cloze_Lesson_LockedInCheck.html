Why this matters 🌍

Last unit, you fixed the two biggest cloze problems you've ever had: the "one hard gap wrecks the rest" cascade, and the mystery of "despite." Both held up on the real test — no cascade at all, and "despite" was answered correctly. Today isn't about learning anything new. It's about PROVING those two fixes are really yours for good, the same way an athlete keeps practising a move they've already learned so it never gets rusty. A skill you've locked in and can do automatically, without a fresh lesson every time, is worth more than one you have to relearn.

Before We Start — quick check 🔍

Two quick questions on last unit's two big wins.

1. "___ the long queue, the visitors stayed cheerful." What follows the gap — a thing, or a full idea (subject+verb)?
2. True or false: "If one gap in a passage is hard and you're not sure, the safest thing to do is go back and change your answer on the last two gaps too, just in case."

Both correct? Then the two fixes from last unit are genuinely locked in. Today just keeps them sharp. Next →

Keep it sharp — mixed function check ⚡

Five short sentences, five different jobs. Read each one fully before choosing — these are brand-new sentences, not ones you've seen before.

1. "The library closed early for repairs, ___ students had to study in the hallway instead."
2. "The van broke down twice on the way to the museum. ___, the class arrived just in time for the tour."
3. "One group measured the plant's height every morning, ___ another group tracked how much water it used."
4. "The choir practised for only two weeks. ___, they went on to win the regional prize."
5. "___ a badly sprained ankle, the goalkeeper insisted on finishing the match."

Full passage check — same care, gap after gap 🧩

Reminder
Gaps 1 and 5 both use "despite" — that's on purpose, to keep it fresh. Whatever happens on any one gap, box your answer and give the very next gap a completely clean, fresh look.

Locked in ✅

Punctuation decides the LEVEL. Meaning decides the JOB. "Despite" needs a thing or an -ing word, not a full idea. Box your first choice and move on — every gap gets a fresh look, no matter what happened on the one before it.
