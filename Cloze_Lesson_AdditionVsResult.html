Unit 5  ·  Cloze

Addition vs Result Connectors

Why do students mix these up — and one simple test that fixes the problem every time.
🎯 ISA Priority — Cloze Test
Section 1 of 6

🤔 What Is the Confusion?

You already know the connector families: CONTRAST, RESULT, ADDITION, TIME. But ADDITION and RESULT connectors keep tricking you — here is exactly why.

Both families come after a full stop. Both start a new sentence. They look almost the same in a passage. The only difference is the relationship between the two sentences.

The Exact Mistake

You know what "consequently" and "furthermore" mean individually — but under pressure you grab the wrong one because both feel like "adding something". This lesson gives you a test that makes the choice automatic.

Section 2 of 6

📃 The Two Families

Before the test, let's make the two families crystal clear. Read the descriptions and the example words — then we will build your testing method.

➕

ADDITION — Adding extra information

The second sentence is an extra fact about the same topic. You could imagine the writer saying "and also…" or "on top of that…". The second fact would be true even if the first sentence had never happened.
furthermore, moreover, in addition, additionally,
💥

RESULT — Showing what happened because of it

The second sentence is the outcome of the first. Something in sentence 1 caused or produced sentence 2. If sentence 1 had not happened, sentence 2 would not have happened either.
consequently, therefore, as a result, thus,

Now look at what this feels like in real sentences. The connector is highlighted in colour:

RESULT The match was cancelled because of rain. Consequently, the final score was never decided.
ADDITION The match was cancelled because of rain. Furthermore, the next three games were also at risk.

Same first sentence — different second sentences. One is a result of the rain. The other is just extra information about the rain's impact.

Section 3 of 6

🔑 The "Because Backwards" Test

Here is your new tool. It takes about three seconds, and it will not let you get confused.

🔑 The Because Backwards Test — 2 Steps
1
Read Sentence 2 first. Then add the word "because". Then read Sentence 1. Does it make sense?

Template: "[Sentence 2] because [Sentence 1]."
2
Check the result:
✓ YES — it makes sense

Sentence 1 caused Sentence 2. There is a real cause-and-effect. → Use a RESULT connector: consequently / therefore / as a result

✗ NO — it sounds weird

Sentence 2 is just extra information. It is not caused by Sentence 1. → Use an ADDITION connector: furthermore / moreover / in addition

Let us try it on the two examples from Section 2:

🆕 Try the Test
💥
Example 1: "The score was never decided because the match was cancelled." → Does that make sense? YES ✓ → RESULT → use consequently
➕
Example 2: "The next three games were at risk because the match was cancelled." → Does that make sense? NOT REALLY ✗ — the rain caused the risk, not the cancellation itself. The second sentence is just extra information. → ADDITION → use furthermore
💡 The Quick Memory Tip

If "because" connects the two sentences cleanly → RESULT. If "because" sounds forced or weird → ADDITION.

Section 4 of 6

🔄 Backup: The "Try Both" Method

If you are still unsure after the Because Backwards test, try this as a double-check. Drop both "consequently" and "furthermore" into the gap and read each version out loud in your head.

🔄 Try Both in the Gap

Sentence: "It had been raining for a week. _______, the river burst its banks."

💥
"It had been raining for a week. Consequently, the river burst its banks." → The river burst because of the rain. Makes sense ✓
➕
"It had been raining for a week. Furthermore, the river burst its banks." → Wait — "furthermore" means "and also as a separate fact". But the river bursting is not a separate fact, it is what happened because of the rain. Sounds wrong ✗

When one version sounds like cause-and-effect and the other sounds like a random extra fact, the answer is clear.

Section 5 of 6

📝 Worked Examples — Applying Both Tests

Example 1 — Straightforward RESULT
"Leo forgot to charge his phone before the trip. _______, it died halfway through the journey."
1
Because Backwards: "It died halfway through the journey because Leo forgot to charge it." → Does that make sense? YES — that is exactly why it died.
2
→ RESULT connector.
✓ Answer: Consequently, it died halfway through the journey.
Example 2 — Straightforward ADDITION
"The new sports centre has a heated swimming pool. _______, it includes a professional climbing wall."
1
Because Backwards: "It includes a climbing wall because it has a swimming pool." → Does that make sense? NO — the climbing wall is not caused by the swimming pool. Both are just features the centre has.
2
→ ADDITION connector.
✓ Answer: Furthermore, it includes a professional climbing wall.
Example 3 — The Tricky One (looks like ADDITION but is RESULT)
"The team had trained for six months every day. _______, their fitness was exceptional when the tournament began."
1
Because Backwards: "Their fitness was exceptional because they had trained every day for six months." → Does that make sense? YES — training caused the fitness.
2
This feels like ADDITION because both sentences are positive. But the test tells the truth — it is RESULT. Always trust the Because Backwards test, not your gut feeling.
✓ Answer: Consequently, their fitness was exceptional when the tournament began.
