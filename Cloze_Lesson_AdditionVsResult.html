Unit 5  ·  Cloze

Addition vs Result Connectors

Two families that look similar — but mean completely different things. Here is how to tell them apart every time.
🎯 ISA Priority — Cloze Test
Section 1 of 5

🤔 The Confusion

You already know the four connector functions: CONTRAST, RESULT, ADDITION, and TIME. In your last test, you scored well on CONTRAST and TIME — but you mixed up ADDITION and RESULT in several gaps.

Here is why this happens: ADDITION connectors and RESULT connectors often appear in the same position in a sentence (after a full stop), and they both sound like they are "adding something". The difference is what kind of something.

The Core Mistake

Consequently (RESULT) and Furthermore (ADDITION) are NOT interchangeable. If you swap them, the sentence can become logically wrong — even if it looks fine at first glance.

This lesson gives you a one-question test that will tell you which family to use every single time.

Section 2 of 5

📃 Meet the Two Families

➕ The ADDITION Family

Use when the second sentence adds extra information — a new fact, feature, or detail. The second idea is not caused by the first. Both ideas are just true at the same time.
furthermore, moreover, in addition, additionally, also,

💥 The RESULT Family

Use when the second sentence is an outcome or effect of the first. The first idea causes the second idea. Without the first event, the second would not happen.
consequently, therefore, as a result, thus,
Visual Trick

ADDITION = stacking bricks side by side (more and more facts). RESULT = a domino falling (first event knocks over the second event).

Section 3 of 5

❓ The One Question You Must Ask

When you see a gap between two sentences, ask yourself this single question:

🔑 The Key Question
"Is the second idea caused by the first idea — or would it be true anyway?"
YES — it is caused by the first

The first event produced the second event. → Use a RESULT connector: consequently / therefore / as a result

NO — it would be true anyway

The second sentence is just extra information. It is not caused by the first. → Use an ADDITION connector: furthermore / moreover / in addition

Let us practise this question on two examples that look very similar:

Example A

"The storm knocked out the power lines. ______, the whole city lost electricity."

Ask: Was the city losing electricity caused by the storm knocking out power lines? YES — that is exactly how it happened. → RESULT connector.

✓ Answer: Consequently, the whole city lost electricity.
Example B

"The museum has an ancient Egyptian gallery. ______, it features a collection of Roman coins."

Ask: Was the Roman coin collection caused by the Egyptian gallery? NO — the museum just happens to have both. It is extra information. → ADDITION connector.

✓ Answer: Furthermore, it features a collection of Roman coins.
Section 4 of 5

📝 Worked Examples — Step by Step

Here are three examples. Read each gap, ask the key question, and see how the answer follows logically.

Worked Example 1 — clear RESULT
"The scientist had been studying the volcano for months and noticed unusual activity. _______, the government ordered an evacuation of the surrounding villages."
1
Period test: There is a full stop before the gap. So I need a sentence-starter.
2
Key question: Did the government evacuate villages because the scientist noticed unusual activity? YES — the warning caused the evacuation.
3
→ RESULT connector: consequently / therefore / as a result
✓ Answer: Consequently, the government ordered an evacuation.
Worked Example 2 — clear ADDITION
"The new community centre offers free swimming lessons every weekend. _______, it runs a popular after-school homework club for local children."
1
Period test: Full stop before the gap. Sentence-starter needed.
2
Key question: Is the homework club a result of the swimming lessons? NO — the centre just happens to offer both. They are separate programmes.
3
→ ADDITION connector: furthermore / moreover / in addition
✓ Answer: Furthermore, it runs a popular after-school homework club.
Worked Example 3 — the tricky one
"The expedition team had trained rigorously for eight months. _______, their fitness levels were exceptional when they reached base camp."
1
Period test: Full stop before the gap. Sentence-starter needed.
2
Key question: Were their exceptional fitness levels caused by eight months of training? YES — the training produced their fitness. This is a direct cause-effect.
3
→ RESULT connector. (Note: it might look like ADDITION because both facts are positive. But ask the key question — the fitness is a direct outcome of the training.)
✓ Answer: Consequently, their fitness levels were exceptional when they reached base camp.

🎉 All 5 Questions Complete!

You can now tell ADDITION and RESULT apart using the Key Question. You are ready for Unit 5.

ADDITION Family

  • Furthermore, / Moreover, / In addition, / Additionally,
  • The second idea is NOT caused by the first
  • Both facts are independently true
  • Think: stacking bricks side by side

RESULT Family

  • Consequently, / Therefore, / As a result, / Thus,
  • The second idea IS caused by the first
  • First event → produces → second event
  • Think: a domino falling and knocking the next one over

The Key Question — memorise this

  • "Is the second idea caused by the first idea — or would it be true anyway?"
  • YES → RESULT connector (consequently / therefore / as a result)
  • NO → ADDITION connector (furthermore / moreover / in addition)
