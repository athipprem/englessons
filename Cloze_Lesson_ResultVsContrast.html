What happened in Unit 11

Two gaps swapped: you put however where so belonged, and so where however belonged. Both words were spelled and placed fine — the mix-up was the meaning: caused vs surprise. One quick question fixes it for good.

The "Because / But" question 🤔

When two ideas meet at a gap, ask one thing: can I join them with "because", or with "but"?

If you can say…It is…Words
"…so…" / the 1st causes the 2ndRESULTso · as a result · therefore
"…but…" / the 2nd is a surpriseCONTRASThowever · although
Try it on the Unit 11 gap

"The smell of brownies drifted out, ___ a long queue formed."
Does the smell cause the queue? Yes — people queued because of the smell → RESULT → so (it's a comma, so the joiner word).
It is NOT a surprise, so "however" is wrong here.

Then add the level (you already nailed this)
Full stop before the gap → starter (as a result / however). Comma or mid-sentence → joiner (so / although).

Drill 1 — Caused or Surprise? ⚡

Don't pick a word yet — just decide the link: RESULT (caused) or CONTRAST (surprise)?

1. "The road was icy, ____ the drivers slowed right down."
2. "She practised for hours, ____ she still forgot her lines."
3. "The power went out. ____, the whole street fell dark."
4. "It rained all morning. ____, the match went ahead as planned."
5. "The bridge was closed, ____ traffic was diverted for miles."
6. "Ben is the smallest in his class, ____ he jumps the highest."
7. "The oven was far too hot. ____, the biscuits burned at the edges."
8. "The film got terrible reviews. ____, the cinema was packed."

Drill 2 — Now pick the word 👉

Decide caused-or-surprise, then let the punctuation choose the level.

1. "The alarm rang. ____, everyone left the building." full stop
2. "The cake looked plain. ____, it tasted wonderful." full stop
3. "The tap was left running, ____ the sink overflowed." comma
4. "The puppy was tiny, ____ its bark was enormous." comma
5. "The team trained hard. ____, they won the cup." full stop
6. "The path was steep. ____, nobody complained." full stop

Drill 3 — Snow Day cloze ❄️

Five gaps. For each: caused or surprise (or time)? Then the punctuation picks the level.

Snow fell heavily all night. (1)____, the school bus could not reach the village. Most pupils were disappointed, (2)____ a few cheered at the thought of a day off. Mr Ito opened the gates (3)____ the caretaker cleared the path. Everyone expected lessons to be cancelled. (4)____, the teachers carried on as normal. The sun came out at noon, (5)____ the snow quickly began to melt.

Gap 1 — "Snow fell all night. ____, the bus could not reach the village."
Gap 2 — "pupils were disappointed, ____ a few cheered"
Gap 3 — "Mr Ito opened the gates ____ the caretaker cleared the path"
Gap 4 — "Everyone expected lessons cancelled. ____, the teachers carried on"
Gap 5 — "The sun came out at noon, ____ the snow began to melt"

Your cloze move 📋

StepThe move
1. LinkCaused → RESULT. Surprise → CONTRAST. (Same time → TIME. Extra idea → ADDITION.)
2. LevelFull stop → starter. Comma / mid → joiner.
3. Pickso/as a result (caused) · however/although (surprise).
Because = caused = so.
But = surprise = however.
