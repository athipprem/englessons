Why guessing fails 🚫

Your cloze errors are not a vocabulary problem — you know these words. The problem is identifying which function a gap needs, then choosing the matching word.

❌ Without a method — Unit 8 Q5:

"He grinned at the crowd. (1)___, the audience burst into loud applause."
You think: "meanwhile sounds right here..." → Wrong. −1 mark.

With the 3-step test: "Did the audience applaud BECAUSE he grinned?" → YES → RESULT → consequently. Correct. +1 mark.

Never pick a word that "sounds right."
Identify the function first. Then choose the word.

The 4 Function Families 🎨

Every cloze gap belongs to exactly one function family. Spot the family first — then choose the correct word from that family.

🟠 RESULT
The second idea happens because of the first.
because · consequently · therefore

"She trained hard. Consequently, she qualified."
🟣 CONTRAST
The second idea is surprising or opposite to the first.
although · however · even though

"She trained hard, although she didn't qualify."
🟢 TIME
Two things happening at the same time, often in different locations.
while · meanwhile · as

"She raced while the crowd cheered."
🔵 ADDITION
Extra information — not caused by the first idea, not surprising.
furthermore · moreover

"She qualified. Furthermore, she broke the record."
⚠ Check the structure before anything else
Full stop before the gap? → Sentence-starter → use: consequently · however · meanwhile · furthermore
Comma before the gap? → Clause-joiner → use: because · although / even though · while

The 3-Step Function Test 🧪

Run these tests in order. Stop at the first YES.

Step 1 — Because Backwards
Say the second clause BECAUSE the first clause. Does it make true cause-and-effect sense?
YES → RESULT (because / consequently)
Step 2 — Simultaneous Test
Are both ideas happening at exactly the same time, possibly in different places?
YES → TIME (while / meanwhile)
Step 3 — Surprise Test
Is the second idea unexpected or opposite to the first?
YES → CONTRAST (although / however)
All three NO? → ADDITION (furthermore)

Worked Examples 📖

Example 1 — Comma before gap (clause-joiner):

"The café had just been renovated, (1)___ it was still impossible to find a free seat."

StructureComma before gap?YES — clause-joiner slot
