Why this matters 🌍

"If" sentences let you talk about things that aren't happening — possibilities, dreams, and "what could have been". "If it rains, we'll stay in." "If I had a dog, I'd walk it." "If I had studied, I would have passed." These are everywhere — in stories, in exams, in everyday talk. There are exactly four kinds, and they're called by the same names all over the world. We'll learn the real names — no made-up labels this time.

Fresh start, real names ✅

We're going to use the standard names every teacher and textbook uses: Zero, First, Second, Third. Each one is just a little recipe of verb tenses. Learn the recipes, and the tricky exam questions become easy. Lots of examples coming up. Next →

The four recipes 📋

Each conditional = an if-half + a result-half, each with a set tense. Read the examples aloud.

TypeRecipeExampleWhen you use it
ZeroIf + present, … presentIf you heat ice, it melts.Facts always true
FirstIf + present, … will + verbIf it rains, we will stay in.A real future maybe
SecondIf + past simple, … would + verbIf I had a dog, I would walk it.A dream about now (not real)
ThirdIf + had + past participle, … would have + verbIf I had studied, I would have passed.Looking back at the past
Exam tip — where the marks are
Zero and First are easy; you already say them every day. Almost every tricky exam question is about Second vs Third. So next we zoom right in on those two.

Second vs Third — the big one ⚖️

Second = dream about NOW

Imagining a different right-now. It isn't true, but you picture it.

If + past simple … would + verb

"If I had a robot, it would tidy my room."

Third = look back at the PAST

Imagining the past going differently. Too late to change now.

If + had + past participle … would have + verb

"If I had set an alarm, I would have woken up."

Quickest tell — look at the RESULT half:
plain "would …" = Second  |  "would have …" = Third
Look at the RESULT half
↓
Does it say "would have …" or just "would …"?
"would …" ↓
SECOND → if-half should be past simple ("had a …")
"would have …" ↓
THIRD → if-half should be "had + past participle"

Drill 1 — name the type 🏷️

Read each sentence and pick the conditional.

1. "If you mix red and blue, you get purple."
2. "If it snows tomorrow, we will build a snowman."
3. "If I had wings, I would fly to school."
4. "If she had left earlier, she would have caught the bus."
5. "If a plant gets enough sunlight, it grows."

Spot the error 🔎 — two simple checks

Check 1 — same type. Both halves must be the SAME conditional. Look at the result first: "would have" needs "had + past participle" in the if-half; plain "would" needs the past simple. Mixed up = the error.

Check 2 — the "will" rule. The result of a Second or Third never uses "will". See "will" after the comma → wrong.

Drill 2 — correct or error? 🧦

Use the two checks. Is each sentence correct, or is there an error?

1. "If I had a bike, I would ride to the park."
2. "If they had trained harder, they would have won the cup."
3. "If the room had a window, it would have felt brighter."
4. "If we had left on time, we would catch the train."
5. "If the cake had more sugar, it would taste sweeter."
6. "If the door had been locked, the cat will stay inside."
Name it by the recipe. To check an "if" sentence: look at the result half — "would" = Second, "would have" = Third — and make the if-half match. And "will" is never in the result.
