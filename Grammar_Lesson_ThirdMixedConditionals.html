Third & Mixed Conditionals

Talking about things that did not happen in the past — and what that means for now.
🎯 ISA Priority — Grammar
Section 1 of 4 — Quick Review

📚 The Conditional Family

You already know three members of the conditional family. Here is the full picture, including the two new ones you will learn today.

TypeIf-clauseResult clauseMeaning
Zero If + present simple present simple Always true (facts, laws of nature)
First If + present simple will + base verb Possible future — might really happen
Second If + past simple would + base verb Imaginary present — not real now
Third ★ If + had + past participle would have + past participle Imaginary past — did NOT happen
Mixed ★ If + had + past participle would + base verb Past condition → present result

Today's focus is the two rows marked ★. Both involve the past perfect (had + past participle) in the if-clause.

Section 2 of 4

📋 The Third Conditional — Imagining a Different Past

The Third Conditional is used when something did not happen in the past, and you are imagining what would have been different. Both the situation and the result belong to the past.

Third Conditional Formula

If + [subject] + had + [past participle] , [subject] + would have + [past participle]
The if-clause describes something that did NOT happen. The result clause tells you what the consequence would have been.
If she had studied harder , she would have passed the exam.

Reality: She did NOT study harder. She did NOT pass the exam. This sentence imagines the alternative past.

If they had left earlier , they would have caught the train.

Reality: They did NOT leave earlier. They missed the train.

Key Clue

Both clauses are about the PAST. The if-clause = a past event that did NOT happen. The result clause = the past consequence that also did NOT happen.

Common Mistake

Do not mix tenses randomly. If the if-clause uses had studied, the result clause must use would have passed — NOT would pass. That "would pass" (without "have") belongs to the Second Conditional.

Section 3 of 4

👥 The Mixed Conditional — Past Cause, Present Effect

Sometimes a past event changes your situation right now. The Mixed Conditional connects a past condition (something that did or did not happen) with a present result (how things are today).

Mixed Conditional Formula

If + [subject] + had + [past participle] , [subject] + would + [base verb]
The if-clause is past (had + pp). The result clause is present (would + base verb — no "have").
If he had taken the job offer, he would be living in Paris now.

Reality: He did NOT take the job offer in the past → He is NOT living in Paris now. The past decision affects the current situation.

If they had built the road, the village would be much easier to reach.

Reality: The road was NOT built → The village is still hard to reach today.

How to Tell Third from Mixed

Ask: is the result in the past or the present? If the result is past → Third Conditional (would have done). If the result is present (happening now or still true) → Mixed Conditional (would do / would be).

Section 4 of 4 (Content)

📝 Worked Examples

Identify the type and complete the sentence
If the explorer ________ (not / lose) his map, he would have reached the summit.
1
The result clause says "would have reached" → past result. This is a Third Conditional (past-to-past).
2
Third Conditional if-clause = had + past participle. Negative: had not lost.
3
Reality: He DID lose his map. He did NOT reach the summit.
✓ If the explorer had not lost his map, he would have reached the summit.
Third or Mixed?
If she had practised every day, she ________ (be) a professional musician now.
1
The key word is "now" — the result is happening at the present moment. That is a current state, not a past event.
2
Past condition + present result = Mixed Conditional.
3
Mixed result clause = would + base verb. She would be (not "would have been").
✓ she would be a professional musician now.

Third Conditional

  • If + had + past participle
  • Result: would have + past participle
  • Both condition and result are PAST
  • Used when something did not happen

Mixed Conditional

  • If + had + past participle
  • Result: would + base verb (NO "have")
  • Past condition → PRESENT result
  • Key signal: "now", "today", "still"
