The Cloze Decision Tree 🌳

For every gap, walk down this map once. Two questions, then commit.

START at the gap
↓

STEP 3 — Write your answer, draw a box around it, MOVE ON. Do not rub out.
Why the box matters
Your first map-answer is almost always right. Boxing it tells your brain "decided" — so you stop second-guessing and you don't run out of time. One walk down the tree per gap. That's it.

Drill 1 — Walk Step 1 (Level) ⚡

Full stop before the gap = Starter. Comma or mid-sentence = Joiner.

1. "…the whistle blew. ____, the runners set off."
2. "…the rope was frayed, ____ the climbers turned back."
3. "…she packed the tent ____ her brother rolled the mats."
4. "…the lift was broken. ____, we took the stairs."

Drill 2 — Walk the whole tree 🌳

Step 1 (level) → Step 2 (link) → pick the word.

1. "The sun blazed all afternoon. ____, the team handed out water." full stop
2. "The cake looked burnt, ____ it tasted delicious." comma
3. "Dad cooked breakfast ____ the children fed the rabbits." mid-sentence
4. "The path was blocked, ____ we found another way round." comma
5. "Everyone finished the race. ____, they were given medals." full stop

Drill 3 — Treasure Hunt cloze 🗺️

Walk the tree for each gap. Decide once, box it, move on.

The map was old and torn. (1)____, the children could still make out the path to the cove. They set off early (2)____ the tide was still low. The first clue was hidden under a flat rock. (3)____, the second was tied to a branch high in a pine. Reaching it was hard, (4)____ Mia climbed up without a moment's hesitation. The last clue led them to a small wooden chest. (5)____, the chest held nothing but a note: "The real treasure was the morning itself."

Gap 1 — "old and torn. ____, the children could still make out the path"
Gap 2 — "set off early ____ the tide was still low"
Gap 3 — "hidden under a flat rock. ____, the second was tied to a branch"
Gap 4 — "Reaching it was hard, ____ Mia climbed up without hesitation"
Gap 5 — "led them to a small chest. ____, the chest held nothing but a note"
Two questions, one map.
Decide once. Box it. Move on.
