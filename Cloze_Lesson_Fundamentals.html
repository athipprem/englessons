Unit 1 · Phase 1: Foundation Hardening

Cloze Test Fundamentals

How to read a gap, identify what's missing, and fill it with confidence.
⚠ Priority 1 Gap — ASMOPSS baseline: 10/20 (50%)
Section 1

📖 What is a Cloze Test?

A cloze test gives you a passage with words removed. Your job is to figure out the missing word using the meaning and grammar of the surrounding text.

Think of it like a puzzle: the words around the gap are your clues. You are not guessing — you are reasoning.

The Core Idea

Every gap has exactly one best answer. The passage tells you what it is — if you read carefully enough.

Cloze tests appear in ISA Reading and in competitions like ASMOPSS. They test whether you understand how language flows — not just individual words in isolation.

Section 2

🔍 The 4-Step Strategy

  1. Read the whole passage first. Do not stop at the first gap. Get the overall meaning before trying to fill anything in.
  2. Identify the part of speech. Ask: is the missing word a noun, verb, adjective, adverb, or connector? The grammar of the sentence tells you.
  3. Use context BEFORE and AFTER the gap. The sentence before it and the sentence after it both give clues. Never only look at one side.
  4. Say the sentence aloud (in your head). Does it sound right? Does it make sense with the whole passage? If yes — that's your answer.
Common Mistake

Reading only the words immediately next to the gap. The sentence after the gap often explains what the missing word should be. Always look both ways.

Section 3

🆕 Identify the Part of Speech First

Before thinking about which word to use, decide what type of word is needed. This cuts your options down dramatically.

Noun
What is it? / Who is it?
city, scientist, decision, discovery
Verb
What does it do?
runs, discovered, has been, created
Adjective
What is it like?
dark, ancient, careful, enormous
Connector
How do the ideas link?
however, although, because, therefore

How to spot what's needed

Look at the words around the gap:

  • 📌 The ___ was broken. → The word after "The" is a noun (The bridge was broken.)
  • 📌 She ___ her homework every night. → Subject + gap + object → verb (She completes her homework.)
  • 📌 The room was surprisingly ___. → After "was" and an adverb → adjective (The room was surprisingly quiet.)
  • 📌 She studied hard; ___, she still failed. → Joins two contrasting ideas → connector (however)
Section 4

🔗 Connector Words — Know These Cold

Connectors are the most common cloze gap type. The meaning of the gap depends entirely on the relationship between the two ideas.

Relationship Connector Words Example
Contrast / Surprisehowever, although, despite, yet, butHe trained every day; however, he lost.
Cause / Resultso, therefore, as a result, consequentlyIt rained heavily, so the match was cancelled.
Additionfurthermore, in addition, also, moreoverShe speaks French; moreover, she speaks Thai.
Sequence / Timefirst, then, next, finally, afterwardsFirst, mix the flour. Then, add the eggs.
Example / Clarificationfor example, such as, in other wordsShe enjoys sports, such as swimming and cycling.
The Contrast Trap

When two ideas in a passage are opposite in meaning, the gap is almost always a contrast connector. Look for clue words like "but", "still", "yet", "despite" — even if the gap is in a different part of the sentence.

Section 5

✏️ Worked Examples

Let's walk through the 4-step strategy together. Watch how each step narrows down the answer.

Example 1 · Foundational

The weather was cold and rainy, ________ the children decided to stay inside and play board games.
1
Read the whole sentence. There are two ideas: bad weather and staying inside.
2
The gap joins two clauses → it needs a connector.
3
The weather is bad → staying inside is a logical result of bad weather. This is a cause-and-effect relationship.
4
Cause/result connector needed → so fits perfectly.
✓ Answer: so  (cause → result connector)
Example 2 · Developing

The scientist worked for ten years on her research. Her greatest ________ came when she proved that the ancient ruins were over 3,000 years old. People around the world celebrated her work.
1
Read all three sentences. The passage is about a scientist who worked hard and achieved something great.
2
"Her greatest ___" → after "greatest" we need a noun (Her greatest something).
3
Look after the gap: "came when she proved…" — this was a moment of finding something out. Look at "People celebrated her work" — this was a positive, significant event.
4
A noun for a great positive finding in science → discovery fits perfectly.
✓ Answer: discovery  (noun — greatest discovery)
Example 3 · Developing — The Contrast Pattern

Maria had practised the piano every single day for six months. ________, on the day of the concert, her hands were shaking and she forgot the opening notes.
1
Two sentences: lots of practice → but then things went wrong at the concert.
2
The gap is at the start of the second sentence → it needs a connector.
3
You would expect practice to lead to a good performance. But the opposite happened. This is a contrast/surprise relationship.
4
Contrast connector → However fits. (Note: capital H because it starts a sentence.)
✓ Answer: However  (contrast connector — despite practice, something went wrong)
Section 6 · Practice

🏋 Your Turn

Read each passage carefully. Use the 4-step strategy: read all, identify part of speech, use context before AND after, check it sounds right.
Practice 1 Foundational
Every morning, the baker arrived at his shop before sunrise. He ________ the ovens and began preparing the dough for the day's bread.
Practice 2 Foundational
The hikers had been walking for hours. They were exhausted and their legs were heavy. ________, when they finally reached the top of the mountain, all the tiredness seemed to disappear.
Practice 3 Developing
Dr Patel had dedicated her entire career to finding a cure for the rare disease. After years of failed experiments, she finally made a ________ that would change the lives of thousands of patients worldwide.
Practice 4 Developing
The ancient temple had stood in the middle of the jungle for over a thousand years. It was ________ by thick vines and tall trees, which had slowly grown over its stone walls.
Practice 5 Advanced
The young violinist had performed at hundreds of concerts across the country. Her talent was ________ — critics and audiences agreed that someone of her age had rarely played with such maturity and feeling.
Lesson Summary

🏆 What to Remember

🔍 4-Step Strategy

  • Read the whole passage first
  • Identify the part of speech
  • Use context before AND after the gap
  • Say it aloud — does it fit?

🔗 Connector Patterns

  • Contrast → however, although, yet
  • Cause/Result → so, therefore
  • Addition → moreover, furthermore
  • Sequence → first, then, finally

⚠ Watch Out For

  • Only reading one side of the gap
  • Choosing the first word that sounds OK
  • Ignoring the overall passage meaning
  • Missing the contrast/surprise signal

🎯 ISA Relevance

  • Cloze appears in ISA Reading section
  • Tests language flow — not word lists
  • Basic-level gaps must be 100% reliable
  • Phase 1 goal: no Basic-level misses
