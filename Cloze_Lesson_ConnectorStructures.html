Unit 4  ·  Cloze

Connector Structures

Why "however" and "although" are NOT interchangeable — even though both mean CONTRAST.
🎯 ISA Priority — Cloze Test
Section 1 of 4

🤔 The Problem

You already know the four connector functions: CONTRAST, RESULT, ADDITION, and TIME. That knowledge is real. But there is a second layer that trips up many students — and it is the reason why choosing the wrong word in a cloze test can happen even when you know the function correctly.

The Hidden Trap

However and although both signal CONTRAST — but they work in completely different sentence structures. If you put one where the other belongs, the sentence breaks.

This lesson teaches you the structural type of each connector: whether it starts a new sentence or joins two clauses inside one sentence. Once you know this, you have a reliable check that works on every cloze passage.

Section 2 of 4

📃 Two Types of Connectors

● Sentence-Starters

These come after a full stop (.) or semicolon (;) and start a brand-new sentence. The two sentences can stand alone.

However, Therefore, Furthermore, Meanwhile, Consequently, Nevertheless,

● Clause-Joiners

These sit inside one sentence and join two clauses together. The clause they introduce cannot stand alone as a sentence.

although because while whereas since even though
Key Difference

Sentence-starters need a capital letter and a comma after them. Clause-joiners are lowercase and work inside a single sentence that already has its main clause.

Section 3 of 4

🔎 The Period Test — Your Secret Weapon

Before you choose a connector in any cloze passage, ask yourself one question first:

⚙ The Period Test

.
Is there a full stop or semicolon before the gap?
→ Sentence-Starter group: However / Therefore / Furthermore / Meanwhile / Consequently
→
Is the gap inside one sentence with no full stop before it?
→ Clause-Joiner group: although / because / while / whereas / since

Once you know which group you need, then apply your function test (CONTRAST / RESULT / ADDITION / TIME) to pick the exact word.

Sentence-Starter Structure

Sentence 1 full stop. Connector, + sentence 2.
The experiment failed three times. However, the scientist did not give up.
The team worked all night. Therefore, they finished on time.
She scored full marks on the reading test. Furthermore, her writing improved.

Clause-Joiner Structure

Connector + clause 1, main clause.
Main clause + connector + clause 2.
Although the experiment failed three times, the scientist did not give up.
The team finished on time because they worked all night.
While her reading was excellent, her writing still needed practice.
Quick Test

Try reading just the connector's clause on its own. "Although the experiment failed three times." — that cannot stand alone. It is a clause-joiner. "However, the scientist did not give up." — that stands alone perfectly. It is a sentence-starter.

Section 4 of 4 (Content)

📝 Worked Examples — Two Steps

Use the period test first, then the function test. Watch how both steps work together.

Example 1
The hikers were exhausted after climbing all day. ________, they decided to set up camp early.
1
There is a full stop before the gap → I need a sentence-starter. Clause-joiners (although, because, while) are eliminated immediately.
2
What is the logic? The hikers were exhausted → they set up camp early. That is a RESULT (tired, so they stopped). I need a result sentence-starter.
3
From the sentence-starter group: Therefore = RESULT. Therefore fits.
✓ Therefore, they decided to set up camp early.
Example 2
________ the storm was severe, no buildings were damaged.
1
The gap is at the start of the sentence with no full stop before it → I need a clause-joiner. Sentence-starters (However, Therefore) are eliminated.
2
What is the logic? Severe storm — but no damage. That is a surprising CONTRAST (you would expect damage, but there was none).
3
From the clause-joiner group: Although = CONTRAST. Although fits perfectly.
✓ Although the storm was severe, no buildings were damaged.
Example 3 — Tricky
Maya studied every night for three weeks. ________, she still found some questions difficult.
1
Full stop before the gap → sentence-starter required. Although is out.
2
Logic: She studied a lot — but still found it difficult. Opposite of what you would expect → CONTRAST.
3
From the sentence-starter group: However = CONTRAST. However fits.
✓ However, she still found some questions difficult.

Sentence-Starters

  • Come after a full stop (.) or semicolon (;)
  • Start a new, complete sentence
  • Always followed by a comma
  • However / Therefore / Furthermore / Meanwhile / Consequently / Nevertheless

Clause-Joiners

  • Live inside one sentence — no full stop before them
  • The clause they introduce cannot stand alone
  • No comma needed directly after them (comma may come later)
  • although / because / while / whereas / since / even though

The Two-Step Method

  • Step 1 — Period Test: full stop before gap? → sentence-starter. No full stop? → clause-joiner.
  • Step 2 — Function Test: CONTRAST / RESULT / ADDITION / TIME?
  • Pick the word that satisfies BOTH steps.
